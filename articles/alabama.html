Cloudmont DH MTB Park: Gravity Comes to Alabama

Did it snow today?

Lift-access DH is coming to the Deep South.

What was once an unassuming, weather-dependent ski hill in Mentone, Alabama is about to become a major destination for mountain biking. A bold project is transforming Cloudmont Ski and Golf Resort—the southernmost ski area in the US—into Alabama’s first lift-served downhill mountain bike park.

For those of you who regularly drive four hours to North Carolina or Tennessee just to ride gravity trails, your weekends just freed up. Cloudmont isn’t just another trail system; it’s a fundamental shift in the Southern riding hierarchy.

The Stats:

  • Vertical Drop: 150 ft
  • Summit Elevation: 1,800 ft
  • Humidity: Yes
  • Lifts: 2 retrofitted surface lifts (pony lifts)
  • Location: Lookout Mountain (Mentone, AL) — 2 hours from ATL, 1.5 hours from HSV.
  • Season: Nearly Year-Round

The footprint of Cloudmont is condensed. This geographical constraint makes the developments even more impressive—it’s maximizing vertical space because horizontal space doesn't exist.

Sure, 150 feet might sound like a speed bump to the Trestle or Whistler crowds, but let’s look at the facts: a perfectly engineered flow trail, progressive drop zone, or dirt jump line doesn't need alpine elevation to send you to the moon. They are making impressive use of "stacked loop" trail layouts to put high-quality features in a tight vertical footprint.

Transformative Economics

When a community invests in mountain bike trails it's not a sunken cost. The community makes back that investment in many ways: more restaurants, more jobs, more cups of coffee, more tax revenue...

Mentone is a quintessential Appalachian mountain town with cabins, BBQ, and charm. Adding a year-round gravity draw is going to supercharge that local economy. I suspect the local businesses will agree trails are good business.

Transforming Cloudmont into a year-round downhill mountain bike park presents a massive economic windfall for Mentone and the surrounding DeKalb County area. According to extensive economic impact studies from organizations like the International Mountain Bicycling Association (IMBA), destinations that invest in dedicated mountain bike infrastructure see a substantial, measurable influx of out-of-state visitor spending. Because Cloudmont sits within a two-hour drive of major metro hubs like Atlanta and Huntsville, it is primed to capture a high-volume influx of weekend travelers who don't just buy lift tickets—they fill local cabins, frequent area restaurants, buy fuel, and support small retail businesses. By creating a reliable, year-round recreational anchor, this park converts natural terrain into a powerful economic engine that drives tax revenue, generates steady hospitality jobs, and solidifies Lookout Mountain as a premier outdoor adventure destination in the Southeast.

redfoxrun's take:

After seeing what they did with Virginia Canyon in Colorado, it's clear you can build an incredible experience anywhere if you get creative with the layout. The layout at Cloudmont is legit: bridges, sculpted flow and twisted root tech. This should remind others across the Midwest and South to dream big. How many bike parks are in your state?

This is just fan fiction for now, hopefully the team at Cloudmont will consider Mountain Biking and become a family friendly destination. The mountain biking capital of the south has yet to be crowned.
